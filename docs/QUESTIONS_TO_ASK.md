### 1. About the Role

#### "What does a typical day/week look like for this role?"

**Why Ask**: Understand day-to-day work, work-life balance

**What to Listen For**:
- Balance of coding vs meetings
- On-call responsibilities
- Collaboration style
- Autonomy level

**Follow-up**: "How much time is spent on coding vs other activities?"

---

#### "What are the biggest technical challenges the team is facing right now?"

**Why Ask**: Shows you're thinking about impact, understand what you'll work on

**What to Listen For**:
- Scale challenges
- Technical debt
- Migration projects
- Performance issues

**Follow-up**: "How is the team approaching these challenges?"

---

#### "What does success look like in the first 3/6 months?"

**Why Ask**: Set expectations, understand onboarding process

**What to Listen For**:
- Clear milestones
- Support during onboarding
- Learning expectations
- Contribution expectations

**Follow-up**: "What support is available during onboarding?"

---

#### "What are the key responsibilities and ownership areas?"

**Why Ask**: Understand scope, autonomy, ownership

**What to Listen For**:
- End-to-end ownership
- Cross-functional collaboration
- Technical leadership opportunities

### 2. About the Team

#### "How is the team structured? What's the collaboration model?"

**Why Ask**: Understand team dynamics, reporting structure

**What to Listen For**:

#### "What's the code review and deployment process?"

**Why Ask**: Understand engineering practices, quality standards

## ðŸ’¡ How to Ask Questions

### During the Interview

1. **Listen First**: Questions often come up naturally during conversation
2. **Take Notes**: Jot down questions as they come to mind
3. **Ask Naturally**: Don't read from a list robotically
4. **Be Genuinely Curious**: Show real interest

### Timing

- **Throughout**: Ask clarifying questions during discussion
- **End**: Save 2-3 questions for the end
- **Follow-up**: Can ask more in thank-you email
